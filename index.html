ADITI J. CHANDRASHEKAR

AI for Science

I am passionate about using machine learning to learn more about how the world around us works.

I am interested in the following two questions.
1. How can we build models that effectively capture the underlying dynamics of the brain?
2. How can this understanding inform and enhance our models?

In my ongoing senior thesis, I aim to solve inverse problems using robust equivariant learning in diffusion models. My recent work at the Allen Institute for Brain Sciences involved leveraging lightweight transformer architectures to study Alzheimer's disease. I’ve also worked on improving functional ultrasound imaging using diffusion models, unrolled architectures, and neural operators for use in brain-machine interfaces. My experience spans across various fields, from neuroscience, to autonomous systems and explainable AI (XAI). In my free time I enjoy playing various instruments including the Guitar and Bass Clarinet.
